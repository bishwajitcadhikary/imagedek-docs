# Authentication

## Social Authentication
Open your browser and go to the following URL: [`https://yourdomain.com/admin/system/social`](https://yourdomain.com/admin/system/social) <br/>
Here you can enable or disable the social authentication. <br/>

### Enable Social Login
Open your browser and go to the following URL: [`https://yourdomain.com/admin/system/social`](https://yourdomain.com/admin/system/social) <br/>
Find the `Enable Social Login` option. You will see a dropdown menu. Select the `Enable` option to activate social login functionality.

### Enable Facebook Login
Here you can enable or disable the Facebook authentication. <br/>

#### Step 1: Create a Facebook App
Open your browser and go to the following URL: [`https://developers.facebook.com/docs/facebook-login/guides/advanced/manual-flow/`](https://developers.facebook.com/docs/facebook-login/guides/advanced/manual-flow/). Follow the steps to create a Facebook App. <br/>
Note: You can also use an existing Facebook App. <br/>
Once the app is created, you will receive the `App ID` and `App Secret`. These credentials are required for integration.

#### Step 2: Enable Facebook Login
Go to [`https://yourdomain.com/admin/system/social`](https://yourdomain.com/admin/system/social). Locate the `Enable Facebook Login` checkbox and enable it. Enter the `App ID` and `App Secret` in the respective fields.

### Enable Google Login
Here you can enable or disable the Google authentication. <br/>

#### Step 1: Create an OAuth Client ID
Visit [`https://console.cloud.google.com/apis/dashboard`](https://console.cloud.google.com/apis/dashboard). Follow the steps to create an OAuth Client ID. Ensure you configure the redirect URI to match your application.

#### Step 2: Fill the Required Details
After creating the OAuth Client ID, copy the `Client ID` and `Client Secret`. These will be used to configure Google login.

#### Step 3: Enable Google Login
Go to [`https://yourdomain.com/admin/system/social`](https://yourdomain.com/admin/system/social). Locate the `Enable Google Login` checkbox and enable it. Enter the `Client ID` and `Client Secret` in the respective fields.

### Enable Twitter Login
Here you can enable or disable the Twitter authentication. <br/>

#### Step 1: Create a Twitter App
Visit [`https://developer.twitter.com/en/portal/projects-and-apps`](https://developer.twitter.com/en/portal/projects-and-apps). Click on the `Create App` button and fill in the required details. After creating the app, navigate to the API Keys section to retrieve your `Consumer Key` and `Consumer Secret`.

Callback URL:
```
https://yourdomain.com/oauth/twitter/callback
```

#### Step 2: Enable Twitter Login
Go to [`https://yourdomain.com/admin/system/social`](https://yourdomain.com/admin/system/social). Locate the `Enable Twitter Login` checkbox and enable it. Enter the `Consumer Key` and `Consumer Secret` in the respective fields.

### Enable Linkedin Login
Here you can enable or disable the Linkedin authentication. <br/>

#### Step 1: Create a Linkedin App
Visit [`https://www.linkedin.com/developers`](https://www.linkedin.com/developers). Create a new app by providing the required details, such as the app name and logo. Once the app is created, navigate to the `Auth` tab to retrieve the `Client ID` and `Client Secret`.

#### Step 2: Enable Linkedin Login
Go to [`https://yourdomain.com/admin/system/social`](https://yourdomain.com/admin/system/social). Locate the `Enable Linkedin Login` checkbox and enable it. Enter the `Client ID` and `Client Secret` in the respective fields.
